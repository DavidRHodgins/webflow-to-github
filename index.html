The First 30 Days

The First 30 days will have two main goals: 
Building a connection with the team and gaining a complete understanding of Vercel’s products and the competitive landscape.

‍Building Trust
‍
One-on-One Meetings: Schedule individual meetings with each VDR, VDR leaders AEs and AE Leaders. Gain insights into their experience, strengths, challenges, and perspectives on their individual roles.‍
Team Discussions: Facilitate team discussions and brainstorming sessions to explore where the team is finding success and where there are areas for improvement.‍
Team Events: Organize informal social activities to foster team spirit and build connections outside of work.
‍
Vercel Product and Industry Analysis
‍
Product Deep Dive:
Master Vercel's products and understand how they impact Platform, Infrastructure, Workflow, Storage, Observability, Integrations, Security, and Reference. ‍
Developer Landscape: Research the developer landscape, trends, and communities relevant to Vercel's target market.
Competitive Analysis: Research Vercel's main competitors in the developer tooling space:
Netlify, AWS Amplify, Jenkins, CircleCI and others. Understand where we beat them and how we handle objections when they come up.
‍
Desired Outcome:  Build trust with your team, become an expert in all things Vercel and the competitive landscape.

The Next 30 Days

The Next 30 days will have two main goals: Gaining a complete understanding of the processes and metrics which are most important to the VDR team and VDR Management team. 
‍
‍VDR Metrics and Process Review
‍‍
‍Data Analysis: Deep dive into historical VDR team performance data. Analyze and understand the key metrics where top performers excel. 
‍Process Deep Dive: Analyze the current outreach process. Identify bottlenecks and opportunities for improvement based on data and team feedback.
‍Lead Source Analysis: Understand which sources generate the highest quality leads and easiest-to-convert leads.
‍Hot Lead Identification: Identify barriers to quickly contacting high-value leads.
‍Technology Stack Evaluation: Review the sales engagement technology stack used by the VDR team. Identify opportunities to leverage technology more effectively.
‍
‍VDR Management and Recruiting
‍‍
‍Management Philosophies: Learn from VDR managers what they feel has made them successful in the role and some of the lessons they’ve learned from being in the role.
‍Hiring: Shadow the hiring and recruitment process to fully understand the characteristics of top performers and what we’re looking for in interviews.
‍Performance management: Understand company philosophies around managing employees and historic trends of employee engagement and performance. 
‍Scaling Plans: Analyze company goals and determine a plan for headcount growth and team structure. 

Desired Outcome:  Gain complete understanding the VDR and VDR Manager roles and best practices.

The Final 30 Days

With a strong foundation laid around connecting with the team and understanding the products, competitive landscape, and best practices, it’s now time to coach and mentor the team to exceptional results.
‍
‍Individual Development Plans: Understand each VDRs development goals and implement a regular coaching and mentorship program tailored to them.
‍Setting Goals: Collaborate with your SDRs to set clear, measurable performance goals aligned with Vercel's overall objectives.
‍Regular Coaching: Provide regular and constructive feedback around metrics, call reviews, and email feedback focusing on both successes and areas for improvement.
‍Receiving Feedback: Foster a culture of open communication and ensure the team is comfortable giving meaningful feedback. It's paramount to improving in the role.
‍Celebrating Success: Recognize and reward individual and team achievements to reinforce positive behaviors and sustain high morale.

Desired Result: Achieve exceptional results across the team.
